Five Magic Players to Watch This Playoff Run

Orlando's veteran core and emerging youth have the pieces — but can they make noise against Washington?

BSL Cuffy 1989 Season · Playoff Preview
Eastern Conference · First Round
Orlando #8 Seed · 42-40
vs
Washington #1 Seed · 68-14

The Orlando Magic enter the postseason with a roster built on veteran savvy and youthful upside. After finishing the regular season at 42-40, the Magic drew the short straw — a first-round matchup against the top-seeded Washington squad, who dominated the East with a 68-14 record. If Orlando is going to pull off the upset, these five players will need to deliver when it matters most.

1
Kevin McHale

McHale brings a veteran presence to the court that cannot be overstated. He is still a solid scorer and an even better defender. His blocks per game have always been high throughout his career, and the Magic are banking on that defensive anchor holding firm when the intensity ramps up in playoff basketball. McHale's ability to protect the rim and alter shots will be critical against Washington's interior threats.

2
Dragan Tarlac

It's going to be interesting to see where Orlando deploys Tarlac, as he is arguably the best defender on the roster. He will likely play whichever position allows him to match up against Washington's top big man. His versatility gives head coach flexibility in adjusting lineups on the fly, and his ability to bang in the post while staying disciplined defensively makes him an X-factor in this series.

3
Danny Granger

Granger is still very young and nowhere near his potential. But if the young buck gets hot from the perimeter, opposing teams better take notice. His physical tools are looking decent at this stage of his career, and the Magic are hoping to see him continue to develop in the coming years. If Granger can catch fire in a playoff game or two, he could be the difference-maker Orlando needs to steal a game on the road.

4
T.J. Ford

Ford is one of the top point guards in the league. When the Magic acquired Ford from the Pistons early in the season, they had high hopes he would be the floor general to take them deep into the playoffs. They shortly after picked up Tim Hardaway, who is also a solid guard, though both are getting up there in age. Either way, Ford is still going to be a player to watch. His ability to control tempo, distribute the ball, and pressure opposing defenses will set the tone for Orlando's offensive execution.

5
Joe Barry Carroll

The scoring big man could be a vital piece to this Magic playoff run. He never really got many nights where he logged significant starting minutes, but maybe the Magic's coaching staff will have to do something unconventional to slow down Washington's dominant frontcourt. Will we see Carroll deployed at small forward in small-ball lineups this postseason? His scoring touch and ability to stretch the floor could provide the Magic with a wrinkle that Washington isn't expecting.

Orlando enters the playoffs as a heavy underdog against the league's best team. But with the right performances from this core group and a few breaks going their way, they have the talent to make things interesting. The question is whether the veterans can hold up under playoff intensity and whether the young players can step up in the biggest moments of their careers. One thing is certain: all eyes will be on these five when the Magic take the floor.
